Throughout my internship at **Amazon Web Services Vietnam** from **January 5, 2026** to **April 5, 2026**, I had the opportunity to learn, develop, and apply the knowledge I gained at university in a real-world working environment.

I participated in building the **SmartHire AI** project — a recruitment platform that automates CV extraction and candidate evaluation, where I took on the role of **Backend Developer – Candidate Flow**. I was directly responsible for designing and implementing the CV upload flow (S3 Presigned URL), the asynchronous processing pipeline (SQS → Lambda → Amazon Textract), and the integration of AWS Step Functions for the parallel interview scoring workflow. For the Amazon Bedrock integration, I collaborated closely with the AI specialist in the team to ensure the backend pipeline connected correctly with the AI inference flow. Through this project, I significantly improved my skills in: backend programming (.NET 8), infrastructure deployment with IaC (Terraform, SAM), Event-Driven Architecture thinking, and technical documentation writing.

In terms of work attitude, I always strived to complete assigned tasks to a high standard, proactively explored new technologies without waiting for instructions, and actively collaborated with team members to ensure project progress.

### Areas for Improvement

* **Systems Thinking & Knowledge Organization:** In the early stages of the internship, I was still heavily constrained by the foundational thinking and academic knowledge from university, which led me to approach real-world system problems in an overly rigid way. I need to further develop the ability to think from an overall architectural perspective — understanding data flows, the responsibilities of each component, and how services interact with one another — rather than focusing solely on individual pieces of code.
* **Technical Communication Skills:** I need to improve my ability to articulate technical problems more concisely and clearly when reporting to mentors, particularly when explaining architectural design decisions.
* **Analytical Problem-Solving:** I sometimes approached problems through trial-and-error rather than analyzing the root cause first. I need to build a stronger habit of thoroughly reading AWS documentation and debugging in a more systematic, structured manner.
